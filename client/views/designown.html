The Arm
  1. Put the measuring tape at the starting point of your shoulder seam. Use a finger to hold the tape in place.
  2. Use your other hand to bring the tape to your elbow.
  3. Swap hands, using the first finger to now hold the tape over your elbow.
  4. Use your other hand to bring the tape to where you would like the sleeve to end. As a rule, for normal cuffs, the sleeve will end between your wrist and your knuckles, two-thirds of the way towards your knuckles. If the cuff is a French one, it should end just before your knuckle.
  5. Read the measure.

Shoulder
The Shoulder
  1. Place the beginning of the measuring tape at the seam where the sleeve connects to the body of the shirt.
  2. Place a finger to hold the tape at the seam.
  3. Run the tape along the shoulder under you reach the jutting bone at the base of the neck.
  4. Place your other finger on that bone, holding the tape in place.
  5. Bring your first finger to replace your second finger. Keep holding the tape down on the bone.
  6. Use your free hand now to run the tape along the shoulder to the opposite seam.
  7. Hold the tape down over the opposite seam.
  8. Read the measurement.

The Chest
  1. Determine where the widest point of your chest is. Depending on how you work out (or not), it differs from person to person. As a rule, locate where the nipple is. The widest point of your chest should be just above the nipple.
  2. Run the measuring tape around the widest part of your chest. Make sure that the tape is horizontal to the ground.
  3. Take a deep breath, puff up your chest, and read the measurement. (if you prefer to look good and take shallow breathes while you are in the shirt skip this step and just read the measurements.
